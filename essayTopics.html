Essay Topics

  • Would you consider Ethan to be a strong person and for what reasons?
  • The narrator first meets Ethan when he moves to Starkfield
  • The narrator sees that Ethan is clearly a strong man physically, and has a strong and good morale conscience as well despite being slightly disabled.
  • As the narrator comes to know Ethan and his story he begins to realize that he lacks inner strength however.
  • He sees that Ethan has been mastered by the circumstances in which he has lived and been exposed to rather than having much control over the events.
  • As the reader learns throughout the novel Ethan seems unwilling to act on any of his desires and seems set on living his life and putting aside any personal dreams and goals.
  • He is set in conventional ways and the only way he tries to escape is through suicide, however he fails at this as well.
  • Though he is a good intentioned and strong person, mentally he is weak and his choices because of this cause his tragic downfall and eternal misery on earth.
  • Relate the physical environment of Starkfield and the characters personalities.
  • Starkfield is a bleak and desolate town with the harsh winters and unappealing landscape.
  • The town's environment seems to encapsulate the characters, as they possess similar attitudes and feelings to the bleakness of the town.
  • It seems as if the town seems to suck all the life out of them and oppresses any of their desires and will power.
  • Ethan longs to escape the town as he can see what it is doing to him and his marriage, however he cannot bring him self to break from his traditional ways and seek pleasure for himself.
  • Instead he accepts his fate and the life Starkfield presents him.
  • When he finally does try to escape the entrapment of Starkfield by killing himself, he cannot do that either, and instead further perpetuates the suffering that the town causes him as his accident leaves him trapped in Starkfield for the remainder of his life.

Ethan Frome
