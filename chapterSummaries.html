Chapter Summaries

  • Chapters 1 - 2
  • The novel is told by an outsider in the first person form
  • The narrator was not present for the events of Ethan Frome's story yet tells them anyways
  • The narrator and Frome became acquaintances while working together at a power plant, and that is how the story is learned
  • Frome is described as a very tall man who had been left lame by an accident occurring twenty-four years before the story is told
  • Frome face constantly displays a very harsh expression
  • The narrator became fascinated by Frome while working with him and wanted to learn his story
  • The story flashes back twenty years to a young and confidant Frome
  • Frome walks down the main street of town on a very cold evening
  • Frome thinks to himself about something he learned five years earlier while taking a technological course at a college in Worchester
  • Frome's college experience was cut short however by his fathers death
  • Frome continues walking to a dance at a local church
  • Frome arrives at the dance to pick up Mattie silver
  • Mattie is the cousin of Ethan's wife Zeena, and Frome seems to have developed affection for her
  • Frome is very jealous of the man Mattie is dancing with as Mattie seems to be very happy at the moment and Denis looks at her with a look of ownership over her
  • Frome has seemed to fall in love with Mattie, he is in awe of her beauty and he enjoys his time spent with her where he can have passionate and enlightening discussions
  • His wife is oblivious to his passion for Mattie as she is consumed with her own health problems
  • Frome waits till the dance begins to simmer down before approaching Mattie
  • He makes sure that she doesn't walk home with the man she was dancing with but instead walk home with him
  • They walk arm in arm down back to Frome's house where Mattie stays as well
  • Ethan decides to bring up the fact that Mattie seemed enthralled with the man she was dancing with and remarks that it shouldn't be long before she moves out of the Frome home
  • Mattie shows some hesitation at the idea that she will be leaving and getting married and her attitude pleases Frome
  • As they approach the house Mattie nearly falls but Frome catches her and they for the first time have close physical contact
  • When they arrive at home Zeena is still awake which frustrates Frome as he doesn't want to be seen walking to bed with his wife by Mattie
  • Frome tries to make up an excuse for staying downstairs yet can not come up with something convincible and decides to head to bed
  • Chapters 3 - 5
  • Ethan went to bed, blowing out the candle before he crawled in so as to avoid looking at his wife.
  • As Ethan enters the bedroom he blows out the candle as he does not want to look at his wife when he enters the bed
  • He thinks back to the walk and regrets that he did not kiss Mattie
  • The narrator tells that Mattie was left to fend for herself after her parents died and that when Zeena fell ill she was in need of a care taker and Mattie was able to fill that role
  • Mattie is not really suited as a caretaker and Frome worries that this will eventually frustrate Zeena and lead to Mattie's departure from the house
  • The following day when Frome returns home after being out he is surprised to find his wife is dressed for a trip
  • He learns that she will be leaving overnight to see a new doctor and it will be the first time him and Mattie are home in the house alone
  • Ethan is thrilled by his chance to spend an evening with Mattie and expresses it through a joyous attitude
  • The desolate town of Starkfield has not seemed to put a damper on his life at least for the time being
  • It is learned that Frome and Zeena developed a relationship after Frome's mother died
  • Frome had developed a fear of being alone and therefore asked Zeena to marry him
  • Their relationship does not quite workout however as Zeena becomes sickly and is a hypochondriac
  • She is absorbed with herself and there is no passion in their relationship
  • Frome spends the afternoon running errands in town and he bumps into the man Mattie had been dancing with the night before
  • Frome worries that the man, Denis Eady, is heading to the Frome house to see Mattie
  • Also during his errands he finds Ned Hale and Ruth Varnum sharing a moment of closeness by kissing
  • He is jealous that they can express their love yet he has to hide his love for Mattie
  • When Frome returns home he is thrilled to be alone with Mattie but it soon seems awkward and at dinner they can not seem to make conversation
  • Frome solves the situation by picking up the pieces and vowing to fix it which calms Mattie
  • Following dinner the two sit by the fire and try to converse however it becomes awkward again after a while as Frome mentions the couple he saw that day in hopes to spark something between them
  • Frome then brings up his dismay over Mattie's eventual departure and she seems to blush
  • They finish the nightly chores after a somewhat awkward evening and both go to bed separately leaving Frome frustrated
  • Chapters 6 - 8
  • All through breakfast, Ethan is inexplicably happy. Although nothing has changed, he feels like he has tasted what life would be like with Mattie.
  • The next morning Frome is extremely happy as he has had a glimpse of what life could be like with Mattie
  • He decides that he will work quickly that day so he can go get some glue and fix the jar before Zeena returns home
  • Frome has trouble finding glue and his return home is delayed therefore not allowing him time to fix the jar before Zeena returns home
  • Zeena has already gone to bed as she apparently is upset about something that occurred on the trip
  • Eventually Frome goes upstairs to call her for dinner but she refuses and tells him that she is in fact sicker than he is aware of
  • She adds that she has been told to rest for months and been told to hire someone to care for her which upsets Frome as he says he doesn't have money for such work
  • He is in fact lying in order to have a reason to keep Mattie around yet Zeena catches him in his lie
  • Frome returns downstairs frustrated as he knows Mattie will be soon leaving and he cannot prevent it
  • At dinner Mattie asks what is wrong but he cannot bring himself to tell her
  • Instead he grabs her and kisses her and at first she returns the kiss yet after a moment withdraws
  • He then tells her that Zeena has decided that Mattie should leave, she now has an uncertain future
  • Zeena eventually comes down for dinner but afterwards has heartburn and goes to seek powder to alleviate it
  • After finding the powder she returns to where Mattie and Frome are with the shards from the broken jar
  • She is furious and Mattie owns up to breaking it causing Zeena to yell and call Mattie a terrible person
  • Frome leaves the room and thinks to himself about what he could do to possibly take back control in his life
  • He thinks back to a couple he has met that had a similar situation
  • The husband left his wife for his lover and the couple moved out west leaving the wife behind
  • Both the couple and the wife who is left both prospered after the separation and Frome believes that can occur with him and Mattie
  • He begins to write a letter to his wife telling of his plans to leave her
  • He however begins to realize that this could not work as Zeena surely couldn't prosper after he left as she is no where near healthy enough, and he doesn't have the money to move out west with Mattie
  • The following day a man arrives to gather Mattie's things and move her away
  • Frome is dismayed as he is helpless now
  • He realizes that if he asks to borrow money to hire someone for Zeena he can take the money and run away with Mattie
  • He then realizes that this plan is not moral as he will leave Zeena helpless and be stealing from people he respects
  • Chapters 9 - 10
  • Ethan returns home and goes upstairs to help Mattie bring down her trunk. He finds her crying.
  • Frome decides to help Mattie pack her things and he finds her in tears in her room
  • After lunch he decides that he will take Mattie to the station where she will depart by himself
  • He takes extra time to make sure he can be alone with her for quite a while
  • On the way they stop at Shadow Pond where the two talk for a while about their affection for one another
  • Frome asks her about her future plans and is saddened that her outlook looks bleak
  • Frome tells her that he would love to run away with her but can't afford it, and he asks if she would even agree if he could
  • She tells him she would but that changes nothing
  • He decides to take her sledding before they leave
  • Mattie tells him that she wants to die with him and they agree to take the sled down the hill in the path of a large tree
  • After the collision with the tree Frome comes to and realizes that neither has died
  • Frome is left holding an injured Mattie in his arms
  • The novel switches back to the present and the narrator is entering Ethan Frome's house
  • Once inside he meets two women, one a tall and very sever while the other is slight and complaining
  • They are both sitting at a table and the tall one stands up to set the table, the other one moves her head but she cannot move anything else, she is paralyzed
  • The narrator realizes that the tall woman is Zeena and the other one is Mattie
  • It is learned that following the collision with the tree Zeena agreed to take Mattie back in and ever since she has been caring for Mattie and Frome
  • Zeena had persevered to take care of them even though at times she though she couldn't take care of herself
  • It seems as perhaps the Frome's would have been better off if Mattie had died, as ever since the accident the life they live has been very troubled and in no way pleasing
